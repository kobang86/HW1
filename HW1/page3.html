The Collections From The Past

The ones that I had to let go of

Jackson Customshop SL2H
"Bengal Tiger"

This beautiful axe is from Jackson Customshop, which swept the 80's glam metal scene with Soloist and Dinky models. I've always wanted to have a tiger-striped guitar and I got this especially for the show that my band had back in May 2015. The stage presence, gain, tone and everything else was great but the only problem was that I had so many metal guitars. Also, I realized I don't look that cool like those hair metal bands from 90's without the spandex and outrageous hairstyles. I had to let this one go to a guy who gives guitar lessons for living.

Suhr Custom Modern "Frost Drip"

John Suhr from California makes great guitars. He used to work for Fender Customshop back in the days. This particular one is custom Modern model with HSH (Aldrich-ML-Aldrich) pickups and finished unique painting called "Frost drip". I still remember the day when I went to the guitar shop in Gangnam area in Korea, tried it for a few minutes and made my decison. It was the first time I bought a new guitar from a guitar shop and it was ridiculously expensive. (I usually buy mint condition used guitars) I bought this because of the finish but soon I got bored with it and sold it to a mid-40 guy who claimed to be a professional player. I don't know why I sold it or bought it from the first place. I think we were just not meant to be.

James Tyler Studio Elite HD
"Alien Guano"

I think I was a little too bored with my life when I first saw this on the internet. I love the brand, the model and the finish but I really did not need another guitar at that time. Everything was just so spontaneous. It was Sunday 11pm and when I texted the seller, he said he would meet me at the halfway and take about $300 off. (He lived at the South end of Korea, which was about 5-6 hours away by car) I hit the road right away. We met at the gas station by the highway. I bought it. I drove home and it was already 3am in the morning and I had to go to work in 3 hours. So I decided to play guitar for 2 hours, take shower and go to work the next morning. I went through all that trouble buying the guitar but then, a few weeks later, I sold the guitar to another collector.
